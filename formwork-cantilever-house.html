01 — CONCEPT
A House Engineered, Not Just Built

A precast concrete frame that cantilevers over its own ground floor.

The upper level reads as a single timber-lined box, pushed forward on a precast concrete and beam structure so the glazing below can run uninterrupted. It's a simple move that does a lot of work: shade for the terrace, privacy for the bedroom above, and a silhouette that doesn't need decoration to be noticed.

Downstairs, the dining and living spaces sit behind full-height, double-glazed timber-framed glass that folds the garden into the room. The kitchen stays open to both, so the house never feels like it's asking you to pick a room.

02 — Features

Built around six decisions

Every material and layout choice on this project traces back to one of six commitments made early in the design.

01
Precast Structure
Beam-and-precast concrete construction keeps the frame fast to build and honest in appearance.
02
Timber-Framed Glazing
Double-glazed glass walls set in warm timber frames temper the concrete's coolness.
03
Tailored Interiors
Dining and living furniture fitted to the plan, not dropped into leftover space.
04
Open Kitchen
An open-plan kitchen layout keeps cooking, dining and living in one continuous space.
05
Integrated Lighting
A single lighting system runs through the house, layered for day and night use.
06
Low-Maintenance Facade
Durable exterior materials chosen to weather quietly with little upkeep.
